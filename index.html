To Zamalik ♡

A Letter I Never Wanted To Write

Click the envelope when you're ready.

To Zamalik

Open when you have a quiet moment.

Before anything else, I want to ask for your forgiveness.

If I have ever hurt you, upset you, disappointed you, or wronged you in any way, knowingly or unknowingly, please forgive me.

I mean that sincerely.

For most of my life, things have always been the same.

Work.
Study.
Repeat.

I never really gave myself room for friendships, for fun, or for people.

Then somehow, life introduced me to you.

And through you, I got to know people like Khadija and Leila, people who became meaningful parts of my life.

Looking back now, those moments mattered more than I ever said out loud.

Thank you for every conversation.

Thank you for every random moment.

Thank you for making life feel a little less lonely.

I wish I had more time.

I really do.

Recently, my heart condition has started showing signs that remind me of a chapter I hoped was behind me.

Because of that, I have to be careful.

And because of that, I may make decisions that seem selfish.

Not because I don't value you.

Not because I don't care.

But because I care enough that I never wanted my struggles to become a weight others have to carry.

I first knew your sister back in 2022.

Funny enough, that was around the same time I was first diagnosed.

Years later, we became closer.

Then I got to know you too.

And there was something I realized very quickly.

I found it difficult to say no to either of you.

No matter the day.

No matter the time.

If either of you needed something, I would always try my best.

Because you mattered.

And you still do.

This is not meant to be a goodbye.

I genuinely hope life proves me dramatic for writing this.

I hope years from now we laugh about it.

I hope there are many more memories waiting ahead.

But if things ever go left...

Please understand something.

I tried.

I tried to keep fighting.

I tried to stay strong.

I tried to keep the difficult parts of my life from becoming yours.

And through it all, I cared.

Deeply.

More than I ever knew how to express.

So if one day I am no longer around, or life takes me somewhere beyond your reach, please don't remember me with sadness.

Remember me as someone who was grateful.

Someone who was lucky enough to know you.

Someone who genuinely appreciated having you in his life.

Because I was.

And I still am.

Thank you for being part of my story.

Thank you for every memory.

Thank you for every kindness.

And thank you for simply being you.

Understand, Zamalik 😊

-- Murshid ♡
